The Beginning of Human Science

In 1934, Professor Madman travelled around the world to study medicine from many different continents, including; Chinese herbal medicine, Indian Ayurvedic medicine and a variety of Western medicine. He discovered that there was a common element in these medicines.

Professor Madman invented MADMAN medicine through a combination of medicine from around the world after 30 years of research. MADMAN medicine was invented to be injected to human body for the purpose of granting immortality by augmenting the very DNA of each human cell.

In today's world, this medicine is rare as only limited amounts were able to be produced. Depressingly, humans can still live longer through various factors that actually take effort such as a healthy lifestyle and diet.

Microscopic Miracles

Did you know?

Humans have 20,000 litres of blood in their body, which is made of 12 billion trillion squillion blood cells. Of these 12 billion trillion squillion blood cells, only 5 are needed for survival. The remaining cells are what contribute to the red colour of blood.

At any one vein and/or artery, the amount of blood flowing past can range from 5 millimetres per year to 10 kilograms per minute, depending on whether the person had eaten breakfast or not on that particular day.

Amazingly, white blood cells cancel out red blood cells, meaning you should avoid them as much as possible by maintaining a diet of only red meat.
